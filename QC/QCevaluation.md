
### Field-by-field interpretation

**Genome length**  
Estimated **haploid genome size**.
Used for memory planning and sanity checks against related species.

**Heterozygosity (%)**  
Estimated fraction of heterozygous sites.
Higher values imply increased graph complexity and haplotig formation.

**Repeat length / Unique length**  
Partition of the genome into repetitive and single-copy sequence.
Explains limits on contiguity and expected assembly completeness.

**Error rate (%)**  
Sequencing error inferred from low-frequency k-mers.
For PacBio HiFi data, this is typically <0.5%.

**k-mer coverage**  
Effective depth of homozygous sequence.
HiFi assemblies generally perform well at ≥25–30×.

**Model fit (%)**  
Percentage of k-mers explained by the GenomeScope model.

| Model fit (%) | Interpretation |
|--------------|----------------|
| ≥95 | Excellent |
| 90–95 | Good |
| 80–90 | Moderate |
| <80 | Investigate data quality or ploidy |

Model fit is a **diagnostic confidence metric**, not proof of biological correctness.

---

## Choice of k for PacBio HiFi

Recommended values:

- **k = 21** — primary estimate (robust)
- **k = 31** — confirmation and repeat sensitivity

Consistent estimates across k values indicate reliable inference.

---

## What this step does *not* do

k-mer evaluation does **not**:

- assemble reads
- resolve structural variants
- identify repeat types explicitly
- replace assembly-level QC

It is a **diagnostic and planning step**, not an assembly step.

---

## How k-mer evaluation guides downstream assembly

| Observation | Assembly implication |
|------------|---------------------|
| High heterozygosity | Expect haplotigs; plan purging |
| Large repeat fraction | Reduced contiguity |
| Low coverage | Fragmented assembly |
| Low model fit | Inspect reads before assembly |
